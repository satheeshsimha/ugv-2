\textbf{This is Problem-2 from Exercise 7.3.2}
\begin{enumerate}
\item In each of the following find the value of ‘k’, for which the points are collinear.

\item (7, –2), (5, 1), (3, k) \\
\item (8, 1), (k, – 4), (2, –5).\\

\item \textbf{Solution for problem : 1}

\begin{align}  
\vec{A}=\myvec{7 \\-2},
\vec{B}=\myvec{5 \\ 1},
\vec{C}=\myvec{3 \\ k}
\end{align}
\begin{align}
\vec{D} &=\brak{\vec{A}-\vec{B}} = \brak{\myvec{7 \\-2 } - \myvec{5 \\1 } } = \myvec{2 \\ -3 }\\
\vec{E} &= \brak{\vec{A}-\vec{C}} = \brak{\myvec{7 \\ -2 } - \myvec{3 \\k} } = \myvec{4 \\-2-k}
\end{align}
 If points on a line  are  collinear, rank of matrix is " 1 "then the vectors are in linearlydependent.
For $2\times2$ matrix Rank = 1 means Determinant is 0.
Through pivoting,we obtain
\begin{align}
\vec{F} &={\myvec{\vec{D}\\ \vec{E}}}
\end{align}
\begin{align}
\begin{split}
\myvec{
2 & -3
 \\
4 & -2-k 
}
\end{split}
\end{align}

\begin{align}
\label{eq:chem_balance_mat_row}
\myvec{
2 & -3
\\
4 & -2-k
}
 \xleftrightarrow[]{R_2 = R_2-2R_1}
\myvec{
2 & -3
\\
0 & -k+4
}
\end{align}
If the rank of the matrix has to be 1 , then -k+4=0.

\begin{align}
\begin{split}
-k+4 &=0
\\
k=4
\end{split}
\end{align}

 \item \textbf{Solution for problem :2}
 
\begin{align}  
\vec{A}=\myvec{8 \\ 1},
\vec{B}=\myvec{k \\ -4},
\vec{C}=\myvec{2 \\ -5}
\end{align}
\begin{align}  
 \vec{D} &=\brak{\vec{A}-\vec{B}} = \brak{\myvec{8 \\1 } - \myvec{k \\-4 } } = \myvec{8-k \\ 5 }\\
\vec{E} &= \brak{\vec{A}-\vec{C}} = \brak{\myvec{8 \\ 1 } - \myvec{2 \\-5 } } = \myvec{6 \\6}
\end{align}
If points on a line  are  collinear, rank of matrix is " 1 "then the vectors are in linearlydependent.
For $2\times2$ matrix Rank = 1 means Determinant is 0.
Through pivoting,we obtain
\begin{align}
\vec{F} &={\myvec{\vec{D}\\ \vec{E}}}
\end{align}
\begin{align}
\begin{split}
\myvec{
8-k & 5
 \\
6 & 6
}
\end{split}
\end{align}
\begin{align}
\label{eq:chem_balance_mat_row}
\myvec{
8-k & 5
\\
6 & 6
}
 \xleftrightarrow[]{R_1 = 3R_1-3R_2}
\myvec{
24-3k & 15
\\
6 & 6
}
\\
\xleftrightarrow[]{R_2 = 5R_2-2R_1}
\myvec{
24-3k & 15
\\
0 & 0
}
\end{align}
If the rank of the matrix is 1, it means any one of the row must be zero.So, making the first element in the matrix equal to 0.
\begin{align}
\begin{split}
 5(6)-2(24-3k)&=0
\\
30-48+6k&=0
\\
k=3
\end{split}
\end{align}
